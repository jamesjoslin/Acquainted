About


Acquainted is my personal project to learn how to engage my community, serve, and make authentic connections. I believe that what makes us human is our desire to be fully known and loved. Acquainted, this blog, (which is by no means the first of its kind,) uses the unique power of encountering a stranger in order to both know and love individuals. Strangers have a unique vantage point, because they can side-step our fear of judgement and shame. A positive encounter with a stranger can have huge consequences our lives! I personally had an encounter with a stranger, and it changed the trajectory of my entire life. We are now the closest of friends, and He showed me I have that the ability to change lives too.

The great thing about this project is that it doesn't stop at a greeting. Acquainted is only a success if it is inviting people into a life-long process of being fully known and loved, and teaching others to do the same. The end goal is a formed relationship.

Acquainted is my way of using my gifts to lead others. If you are interested in gaining resources to do something similar, I would suggest heading to the resources page.







Mission Statement:


"To invite everyday people into communities that love Jesus by invitational and challenging interviews and intentional social introductions."



Vision Statement:


"To have Christianly built communities that desire to know Jesus, think christianly, and serve in the manner that he does." (Adapted from the manuscript of Mark, 3:13-14)









Core Values:


  • LOVE: an unconditional commitment to go out and serve my neighbor's highest good. (1 John 4:7-11)

  • INVITATION: to join community and be given resources to disciple as well. (2 Timothy 2:2, Matthew 28:19)

  • TRUTH: also rationality - the framework for clear thinking, responsiveness, and leading others wisely. In other words, truth is the key that enables others to achieve the classically defined happiness. (Matthew 7:24-25)

  • GRACE: unmerited favor and kindness. Grace and truth is what makes an individual see and know God. (Romans 6:14)

  • SPEECH: a standard of orally/aurally relating to others which seeks the heart of matters in order to invite and challenge. (Colossians 4:6, James 1:19, Proverbs 12:25, Titus 2:7-8, and the historical accounts of Jesus)
